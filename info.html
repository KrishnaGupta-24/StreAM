Stress is the body's natural response to any demand or challenge, whether real or perceived. It is a psychological and physiological reaction that occurs when individuals feel they cannot cope with the pressures placed on them, leading to a state of heightened alertness and physical changes in the body. Stress can arise from various sources, such as work-related pressures, relationship issues, financial difficulties, or major life events like moving, illness, or loss.

Common causes of physical stress include intense exercise, heavy lifting, prolonged periods of physical labor, or even simple activities done without proper rest and recovery.
